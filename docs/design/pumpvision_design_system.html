Numbers worth trusting.

A field-grade interface for the petrol pump — built on the language of the totalizer, the receipt, and the shift register. Light, warm, precise. Made to be read in sunlight, used with one hand, and trusted at the end of every shift. We are pulling something stuck in the past into modernity, without the clean break.

02 · Wordmark

Three readings of the totalizer.

A / B / C

Each option treats the wordmark as a mechanical instrument. We're carrying tradition into modernity — the dial, the cell, the dispenser face — without nostalgia or pastiche.

A · Compressed display + saffron marker
pumpvision

Squarish, vertically stretched lowercase. The saffron square reads as a status LED — small, deliberate, the only color in the mark.

B · Letters framed as totalizer cells

The most literal — each letter housed in a dark digit cell. One cell rendered in saffron as the precision digit. Reads as an instrument, not a brand.

C · Two-tone with meter base ← recommended
pumpvision fuel · operations · trust

The wordmark sits on a meter base — half ink, quarter saffron — that doubles as a fill-level indicator. Readable at small sizes, distinct at large.

03 · Color

Cream paper, ink navy, saffron energy.

Two poles — warm energy and engineered systems — sit on a single warm cream substrate. Saffron appears with restraint, never as a large surface. Ink lives in numbers, rules, and structure. Product colors are a secondary layer, used only to identify fuel grades.

Paper · neutral substrate (warm)

04 · Type

A serif for voice. A grotesk for labels. A mono for numbers.

Three families, used at distinct registers. Newsreader carries the editorial voice; IBM Plex Sans handles UI labels and dense interface text; JetBrains Mono is reserved for any quantity that has to be read precisely. Major Mono Display is a single-purpose instrument — only the wordmark and the totalizer.

05 · Component · Totalizer

The numeric instrument.

Whenever a number carries operational weight — a shift total, a tank reading, a cumulative dispense — it is rendered as a totalizer: each digit framed in its own cell, monospaced, mechanical.

07 · Motion

The roll, the lock, the snap.

Numbers don't fade in. They roll into place. Motion is the product's most distinctive interaction — it should feel mechanical, cinematic, and resolved.

01
All digits start
Every cell begins rolling at the same moment. Random-looking but synchronized — the system is calculating.
02
High digits lock
Leftmost digits settle first, decelerating from 1200ms onward. Reading becomes legible top-down.
03
Precision snaps
The rightmost digit is last, with a perceptible click into place at ~1800ms.
duration · 1800ms · cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
stagger · digit_index × 90ms (right-to-left settle)
snap · final 80ms uses cubic-bezier(0.6, 0, 0.4, 1.4) for overshoot
